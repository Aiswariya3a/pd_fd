Dataset Description

The dataset utilized in this study is a CSV file containing attributes related to face recognition and student engagement across various colleges. Its primary objective is to compute the engagement score of students by analyzing facial expressions and head positioning in different classroom zones. This data is crucial for evaluating engagement levels across diverse institutions using real-time facial analysis.

Dataset Columns & Descriptions

Column Name Description
face_id Unique identifier for each detected face
region Geographical region where the college is located
college_name Name of the college
zone Student's seating position (e.g., front, middle, back)
emotion Detected emotion (e.g., happy, neutral, distracted)
confidence Confidence score of the emotion detection model
created_at Timestamp when the data was recorded
position.x1, position.y1, position.x2, position.y2 Bounding box coordinates of the detected face
position.center_x, position.center_y Center coordinates of the detected face
pose.pitch, pose.yaw, pose.roll Head pose estimation values
pose.confidence Confidence score of the head pose estimation

Significance of the Dataset

This dataset plays a critical role in understanding and quantifying student engagement by analyzing facial expressions and head movements. It supports various educational technology applications, including automated engagement tracking, classroom behavior analysis, and real-time feedback systems. Leveraging advanced computer vision and AI techniques, it provides educators and researchers with insights into student attentiveness and engagement patterns during lectures.

Moreover, the dataset serves as a benchmark for evaluating the efficiency of vectorized operations in high-performance data analysis. This dashboard compares the performance of FireDucks, Pandas, and Polars in executing full dataset operations—assessing both row-wise and column-wise operations— to determine their effectiveness in handling large-scale data processing tasks.
